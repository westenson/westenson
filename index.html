me in 30 seconds

In 2020 I graduated from the the University of Wisconsin where I studied Finance and Economics with minors in Computer Science and Environmental Studies. I'm a Minnesota native and will be moving to Chicago in September to work as an Investment Banking Analyst at William Blair. I have countless interests, mostly unrelated; currently, I'm curious about healthcare technology. I'm an avid outdoorsman with a love for hiking, skiing, biking, camping, and enjoying nature with others. My biggest concerns for humanity are climate change, wealth inequality, mental health, and our education system.

I love getting to know people, their stories and perspectives, so please email me.

what i'm doing now

this is a now page. i use this to motivate myself and show you what i'm currently focused on.
i was inspired by others.

  • 1 - Home Hobbies

    I'm currently back in my hometown of Minnetonka, MN with my family. I have a great love for the outdoors and have been spending my free time biking, running, camping, and on the lake. A benefit of being home is having access to our beautiful piano, I've been improving my mediocre skills and have been thinking about upping my practice time for some serious improvement; maybe I'll make this its own project soon.

  • 2 - Podcast

    I've got a podcast in the works! Podcasts are by far my favorite medium for news and casual learning. For some time, I've been wanting to start one, and now I have the time to do so! The content will consist of interviews with my very talented friends. First episode is out and a new one released each week!. See project below.

  • 3 - Work Preparation

    I start full-time at William Blair in Chicago as an Investment Banking Analyst in September. I recently took and passed the SIE Exam, and am studying to take the Series 63 and 79 exams in a few weeks.

  • 4 - Book Notes

    One of the most important things that I've learned in recent years is the power of reflection. In a world with so much content to consume, most of it goes in one ear and out the other. The most powerful technique I've found to retain important information is deliberate reflection. That is why I'll be spending time every week for the foreseeable future reflecting on and condensing the notes I've taken on the books I read during college. I hope to keep a permanent database of my notes on this site for myself and anyone who cares to read.
